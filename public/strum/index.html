How it works

Three steps. That's the whole flow.

No course to enroll in. No video to scrub through. Pick it up for five minutes a day, or an hour at a time — whatever works.

1
Pick a name
A first name and a password. That's your account — no email, no signup. Type the same name on another device and your progress follows you.
2
Tune and tap
Open the mic tuner, get your strings in tune, then tap your first chord — Em. Two fingers, two seconds, you're playing.
3
Master, then play
Mark chords as you learn them. Songs unlock as soon as you know the chords they need. By chord four, you're playing real songs.
What's inside

A whole guitar lesson in a single page.

Everything a beginner needs — and not much more.

Mic-based tuner
Pluck a string and the needle tells you sharp or flat. Auto-detects the closest string. No clip-on needed.

Why I built this

For my two daughters — then for everyone.

Strum started as a little weekend project: my two daughters (8 and 12) wanted to learn guitar, and every app I tried was either built for adults with credit cards or buried real teaching under hours of video. So I built the simplest thing I could imagine — a single page that teaches you how to play, with audio you can hear and tap.

It turned into this. It's free, it's open in your browser, and I'll never charge for it. If it helps one more person pick up a guitar, that's a win.

— Jeremiah Daws
Common questions

Yes, but actually — is it free?

A few things worth answering up front.

Is it really free?
Yes. Forever. No ads, no upgrade tier, no premium features. Strum is a personal project — I built it for my kids and put it online for free. Your only cost is a guitar.
Do I need a fancy guitar?
Any acoustic or electric guitar will work. Even a borrowed one from a friend. The tuner uses your device's microphone, so all you need to play along is the guitar in front of you.
What if my fingers hurt?
They will, for the first two weeks. That's calluses forming on your fingertips — totally normal. Practice in short sessions (5–10 minutes), give your fingers a break, and within two weeks the soreness goes away for good.
How young can a kid start?
Strum's small-hand chord voicings work for kids 7 and up on their own. Younger kids can absolutely do it with a parent. The lessons assume zero music background — no piano experience, no reading music, no theory.
How long until I can play a song?
Most learners can play their first 2-chord song within a single session (Em + D = "Horse With No Name"). Real strumming with clean chord changes takes a couple of weeks of daily practice.
What about cross-device sync?
Type the same name and password on another device and your progress comes with you — chords learned, streak, achievements, songs unlocked. Built on Convex; everything's encrypted at rest. No personal data beyond your name.
